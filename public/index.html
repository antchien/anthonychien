About
Let me introduce myself. My name is Anthony and I love building things. I am obsessed with making products the best that they can be, whether its improving upon prior work or building it from scratch.

I have come a long ways to where I am today. I graduated from the University of California, Berkeley with dual science degrees, one of which emphasized in computational modeling. After a stint in academic research, I transitioned into business by getting my MBA from NYU. In this new capacity, I've worked as a consultant, equity researcher, and most recently a VP at a technology startup. It is at this last position that I rekindled my love for coding and development. Once re-exposed, I knew that I needed to pursue it full-time.

My web development skillset includes: Ruby, Rails, Javascript, Backbone.js, JQuery, HTML/CSS. I also have years of experience in C/C++, Java, Lisp, MIPS from undergrad. Feel free to poke around and check out some of my projects and reach out with any inquiries. Enjoy!
Portfolio
  • www.yibs.co
    Social networking site that helps you make bets with your friends
    Single-page user experience utilizing AJAX calls to a RESTful API in Rails. Custom auto-complete friend search and drop-down feature to add friends to bets, ala Gmail. Third-party integrations to broadcast bets to other social media outlets (ie- Facebook). Proper event delegation to avoid memory leaks stemming from over-assignment of handlers.
  • OGmail
    Clone of Gmail and its key features (threading, message collections)
    Backbone application consuming RESTful API in Rails. Utilized Backbone.relational to reduce thread reloading. Made Backbone.Model a subclass so we can try to use existing versions of objects if not too aged.
  • Radio-ActiveRecord
    Clone of ActiveRecord basic functionality
    Implemented associations, querying, persistence, and mass-assignments. Utilized heavy ruby-metaprogramming.
